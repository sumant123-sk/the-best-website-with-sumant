The Legend of Rama and Sita

It was here that the demon king Ravana, ruler of Lanka, became infatuated with Sita and, through deception, abducted her, spiriting her away in his flying chariot, the Pushpaka Vimana.

Devastated, Rama and Lakshmana embarked on a monumental quest to rescue Sita. The Resurrection of Osiris

Osiris was the benevolent god-king of ancient Egypt, who taught his people the arts of agriculture and civilization. His jealous brother, Set, the god of chaos and violence, harbored a deep envy for Osiris's popularity. Set devised a wicked plot. He secretly measured Osiris and had a magnificent sarcophagus made to his exact dimensions. At a feast, he announced that the sarcophagus would be a gift to anyone who could fit inside it. When Osiris lay down to try, Set's followers slammed the lid shut, sealed it with molten lead, and cast it into the Nile River.

Isis, Osiris's devoted wife and sister, and the goddess of magic, was heartbroken. She set out on a long and desperate search for her husband's body. Her journey took her to a distant land, where she found the sarcophagus embedded in a tree trunk that had been used to support a palace. She retrieved the body and brought it back to Egypt, where she concealed it in the marshes. However, Set discovered the body, tore it into fourteen pieces, and scattered them across Egypt to ensure Osiris could never be made whole again.

Undeterred, Isis, with the help of her sister Nephthys and the god Anubis, meticulously searched for and reassembled all the pieces. Using her powerful magic, she breathed life back into Osiris's body. Though he could not rule the living world again, he became the eternal ruler of the underworld, the land of the dead. Isis, through her magic, conceived a son, Horus, who would grow up to avenge his father and rightfully reclaim the throne of Egypt from Set.
